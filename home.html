Get to know the Bieszczady Mountains

Bieszczady Mountains is a part of Carpathian Mountain Range, situated at the south-east Poland. This is the wildest part of the country.
The most beautiful part of the mountains is protected by the Bieszczady National Park and belongs to the Eastern Carpathian UNESCO Biosphere Reserve.

Bieszczady National Park

Is the third largest national park in Poland, bordering Slovakia and Ukraine. Animal life is abundant with several species of endangered animals thriving in the area, among them brown bears, grey wolf, European wildcat, wild boar, European beavers, European otter, and European lynx as well as deer (such as moose) and European bison (over 500 live in the area).

Solina

Lake Solina is an artificial lake in the Bieszczady Mountains region, in Lesko County. The lake was created in 1968 by the construction of the Solina Dam on the San River. It has an area of 22 square km and contains 472,000,000 cubic m of water. It's the largets artificial lake in Poland. It's the best known tourist attraction and popular destination to watersports enthusiasts, becouse of it's great depth, water clarity, and mountainous scenery. The lake has been nicknamed the "Bieszczady Sea".

Greek Ortodox Churches

Centuries-old Greek Orthodox churches are a true landmark of the Bieszczady Mountains, with the majority and most interesting located in the area of Ustrzyki Dolne. Most of them are located within a 4 km radius from each other, so they can be easily visited by bike or on foot. There are 3 main architectural styles of those churches, inspired by Lemko, Bojko and Ukrainian cultures, with most of the temples having a separate additional wooden belfry.
