What to see in the Bieszczady Mountains?

Bieszczady Mountains is a part of Carpathian Mountain Range, situated at the south-east Poland. This is the wildest part of the country.
The mountains are protected by the Bieszczady National Park and belongs to the Eastern Carpathian UNESCO Biosphere Reserve.


Bieszczady National Park


Is the third largest national park in Poland, bordering Slovakia and Ukraine. Is also the home for several species of endangered animals: brown bears, grey wolf, wild boar, European: wildcat, beavers, otter, lynx and deer (such as moose) and bison (over 500 live in the area).

Solina


Solina is an artificial lake in the Bieszczady Mountains in Lesko County. The lake was created in 1968. It is the largets artificial lake in Poland. The lake has been nicknamed the "Bieszczady Sea" andd it is best know from it's great depth, water clarity and mountainous scenery.

Greek Ortodox Churches


Centuries-old Greek Orthodox churches are a true landmark of the Bieszczady Mountains. Majority of them is located in Ustrzyki Dolne. There are 3 main architectural styles of the churches: Lemko, Bojko and Ukrainian. Most of the them have a separate additional wooden belfry.
